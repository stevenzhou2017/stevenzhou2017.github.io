Physical AI
Physical AI integrates artificial intelligence with hardware—such as sensors and actuators—to enable machines, robots, and autonomous vehicles to perceive, understand, reason, and act within the physical world in real-time. Unlike digital-only AI, it bridges the gap between digital intelligence and physical interaction, allowing systems to adapt to dynamic environments.
How Physical AI Works
Physical AI systems use multimodal inputs, including cameras, LiDAR, and sensors, to process data and make decisions.
1. Perception & Modeling: AI understands spatial relationships and physical rules of the 3D world, moving beyond static, pre-programmed instructions.
2. Actionable Intelligence: Algorithms (including reinforcement and simulation learning) enable robots to perform tasks such as grasping, navigating, or manipulating objects, says Physical Intelligence.
Benefits and Key Applications
Physical AI is transforming industries by automating complex physical labor, reducing costs, and boosting productivity.
1. Manufacturing: Adaptive assembly lines and robotic, self-driving forklifts, says The World Economic Forum.
2. Transportation: Safer self-driving cars and autonomous vehicles.
3. Healthcare: Robotic assistants for surgery or patient care.
4. Smart Systems: Robotic agents and intelligent infrastructure for smart grids.
Bayesian Network
Bayesian network is a probabilistic graphical model that represents variables and their conditional dependencies using a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG). It calculates the likelihood of events occurring by combining prior knowledge with new evidence, often used for reasoning under uncertainty in fields like medical diagnosis, AI, and anomaly detection.
Key Components
1. Nodes: Represent random variables (e.g., "Disease," "Symptom").
2. Edges: Directed arrows showing direct dependencies between nodes.
3. Conditional Probability Tables (CPT): Each node contains a CPT, quantifying the effect of parent nodes on the child node.
How a Bayesian Network Works
1. Structure Definition: Variables are linked in a directed graph where edges show dependencies.
2. Probability Assignment: Nodes are filled with CPDs (Conditional Probability Distributions), often using Bayes' theorem to update beliefs as new evidence is observed.
3. Inference: Using algorithms, the network calculates the probability of specific outcomes (e.g., predicting the likelihood of a disease given symptoms).
Types of Bayesian Networks
1. Static Bayesian Network: Standard model representing a single point in time.
2. Dynamic Bayesian Network (DBN): Models variables over adjacent time steps (e.g., Hidden Markov Model).
Key Advantages
1. Handling Uncertainty: Explicitly models uncertainty using probability.
2. Causal Insight: Can represent causal relationships rather than just correlations.
3. Domain Knowledge Integration: Can be constructed by experts or learned from data, or a combination of both.
Applications
1. Medical Diagnostics: Modeling diseases and symptoms.
2. Industrial Fault Diagnosis: Root cause analysis for machinery.
3. Risk Assessment: Determining probabilities of insurance claims or hazards.
4. Machine Learning: Anomaly detection and decision-making.
Ubiquitous/Pervasive Computing
Ubiquitous/Persavasive Computing, also named as Ubi-Comp, is a a very interesting and hot research field. The term of "ubiquitous computing" was coined by Dr. Mark Weiser in 1988 which means the processing of any data at any time and anywhere through any network using any device.
5 As in ubiquitous computing: Any time, Any where, Any data, Any network and Any device.
"Ubiquitous computing names the third wave in computing, just now beginning. First were mainframes, each shared by lots of people. Now we are in the personal computing era, person and machine staring uneasily at each other across the desktop. Next comes ubiquitous computing, or the age of calm technology, when technology recedes into the background of our lives."------Mark Weiser
Ubiquitous Computing Vs Virtual Reality
Ubiquitous computing is roughly the opposite of virtual reality. Where virtual reality puts people inside a computer-generated world, ubiquitous computing forces the computer to live out here in the world with people. Virtual reality is primarily a horse power problem; ubiquitous computing is a very difficult integration of human factors, computer science, engineering, and social sciences.
Mobile Positioning Vs Mobile Location
Mobile positioning and mobile location are sometimes used interchangeably in conversation, but they are really two different things.
----Mobile positioning refers to determining the position of the mobile device.
----Mobile location refers to the location estimate derived from the mobile positioning operation.
Mobile Location Estimation System(MLES)
What is LBS(Location Based Services)
Location Based Services, or LBS, are services provided to clients based on the geographical location information of the mobile device.
What is MLES?
MLES is the acronym of Mobile Location Estimation System. Its mission is to develop an advanced mobile location estimation system based on the existing mobile phone infrastructure. MLES has achieved an average location error of 65 meters in selected areas in Hong Kong. MLES differs from other location systems in that instead of using a single method for positioning, it consists of a brunch of location algorithms. Each algorithm is designed to solve a specific problem domain. MLES uses signal strength for positioning.
Mobile Location Estimation System(MLES) Applications
Elderly Care
According to the HK Police, there are about 700 elderly reported to be missing due to Parkinson disease and memory fading. Since only 1/3 of the cases are reported, the HK Police are estimating over 2000 elderly are missing every year. With the location based service, and with the mobility of the HK police force, the "missing" person should easily be found.
Blind Assistance
According to the HK Blind Union, there are 18,000 blind people in Hong Kong. And getting lost or getting disoriented in the middle of the city becomes a common problem. With an MELS enabled application, these people will know and be known where they are and commune within the city.
Education
An MLES application can be an excellent babysitter to students, especially to kindergarten and primary school students. It can tell you if a school bus is approaching so you can bring your kid to take the bus at best timing, avoiding long time waiting or even missing it. It can also remind you when your kid has arrived at school, or inform you when the kid leaves school earlier.
Tourism
MLES could give tourists a new experience while traveling. How nice it would be when tourists can get the background information of an attraction in their own language when they approach it; and how helpful it would be when they could receive information such as recommendation on where to shop and dine nearby. MLES applications act as a guide to help them explore place more comfortably.
Logistics
Information such as current traffic condition, current location and situation of a vehicle in a fleet etc is crucial to an efficient fleet and resource management of logistic companies. With location based service enabled, this real-time information can be captured by a control centre. Logistic companies then can make use of the information gathered to plan more cost-effective routes of the fleet to serve their customer better. MLES can also provide vehicles of a fleet a better security. An alert system and a tracking system can be installed to the vehicles so that owners the vehicles can be informed by the alert system in case their vehicles are stolen. They can then use the tracking system to locate the stolen vehicles.
